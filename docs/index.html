Your browser, everywhere —
on storage you own.

Konode keeps your bookmarks, tabs, history, and installed-extension list in sync across your browsers — but your data goes to your own Google Drive, GitHub, or WebDAV server. No Konode account, no Konode server, no tracking.

Sync what matters

Turn on only what you want. Nothing is read unless you enable it and grant the matching permission.

Bookmarks
Two-way, folders preserved, deletions that actually propagate — no resurrecting old bookmarks.
Tabs & sessions
Restore another device's open tabs on demand.
History
A synced, de-duplicated browsing-history list.
Extensions
See which extensions are missing on a device, with install links.

Storage you control

Pick one backend and sign in with your own account. Your data is written straight from your browser to it.

Google Drive
Sign in once. Konode only ever touches the files it creates (the drive.file scope) — never the rest of your Drive.
GitHub
A fine-grained token scoped to a single private repository.
WebDAV
Nextcloud, ownCloud, pCloud, Synology, kDrive — any WebDAV server.

Private by design

The whole point: Konode is the pipe, not the destination.

No server, no database
Konode runs no infrastructure. Your data goes only to the storage you chose — we never receive it.
No tracking, no ads
No analytics, no telemetry, no fingerprinting. Ever.
Optional end-to-end encryption
AES-256-GCM: encrypt everything before it leaves your browser, so even your storage provider can't read it.
Open source
The code is public and auditable. Credentials stay on your device; delete your data any time, straight from your provider.

Works on any Chromium browser

Chrome, Brave, ungoogled-chromium, Helium and more. Firefox is on the way.
